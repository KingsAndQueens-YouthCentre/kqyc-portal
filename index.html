About Our Organization

Learn about our mission, vision, and the team behind Kings & Queens Youth Centre

Who We Are

The Kings and Queens Youth Centre is a registered non-profit organization (NPO Registration Number: 284-279 NPO) dedicated to addressing social challenges affecting youth aged 15 to 35 in Lufhereng and surrounding communities.

Our mission is to provide educational support, including homework assistance and career guidance, while fostering a positive, nurturing environment to help combat issues such as alcohol and drug abuse, teenage pregnancy, poverty, and mental health issues such as depression.

Our Vision & Mission

Vision: To become a leading educational organization that shapes and nurtures a positive future generation.

Mission: To provide educational support services, through homework assistance and career guidance, to help combat social issues in Glenridge and neighbouring areas.

Objectives:

  • Offer homework assistance and career guidance
  • Create a supportive community for youth
  • Improve academic performance and social well-being
  • Promote responsible citizenship

Market Position & Analysis

No direct competitors were identified in Glenridge. The Kings and Queens Youth Centre is unique within this region, while other NPOs in the area focus primarily on assisting individuals with disabilities.

Our survey of 177 participants revealed that 34.5% face educational challenges, 11.3% experience loneliness, 7.3% deal with peer pressure, and 6.8% struggle with suicidal thoughts. These findings drive our comprehensive approach.

The nearest competitor is the Boys and Girls Club in Protea Glen Extension 12, which offers face-to-face services and charges a R150 joining fee. Our services are completely free, differentiating us significantly in the market.
